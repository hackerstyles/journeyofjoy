The Irula and Gypsy communities have faced significant challenges and struggles


Here are some key points regarding their suffering

  1. Historical Marginalization:

    Both the Irula and Gypsy communities have been historically marginalized in society, often facing discrimination and exclusion based on their ethnic identity and traditional occupations.


  2. Socio-economic Disadvantages:

    Many members of these communities live in poverty with limited access to education, healthcare, and basic amenities. This socio-economic disadvantage perpetuates a cycle of poverty and marginalization.


  3. Land Rights Issues:

    Land rights are a significant issue for both communities. They often face eviction from their traditional lands due to development projects, conservation efforts, or land grabbing, which further exacerbates their vulnerability.


  4. Lack of Recognition:

    Despite their rich cultural heritage and contributions to society, the Irula and Gypsy communities often lack recognition and representation in mainstream discourse and policymaking.


  5. Social Stigma and Discrimination:

    These communities also face social stigma and discrimination, which affects their access to opportunities and resources. Discrimination may be based on their ethnicity, traditional occupations, or cultural practices.


  6. Healthcare Challenges:

    Access to healthcare services is often limited for these communities, leading to higher rates of preventable diseases and poorer health outcomes compared to the general population.


About

Our mission is to support and uplift the poor individuals within the Gypsy community. We are dedicated to providing essential resources, education, and opportunities to improve their quality of life. By addressing the unique challenges faced by this community, we aim to foster a sense of hope, empowerment, and a brighter future for all.
